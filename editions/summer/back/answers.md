## Expert Insights: Spot the Human

*These insights provide the technical "why" behind the interactive scenarios.*

### Chapter 4 — Active Learning (Hospital X-rays)

**The Core Issue:** Uncertainty isn't a monolith.
In the scenario where the AI flags uncertain X-rays, the expert radiologist is dealing with **Epistemic Uncertainty** (the model hasn't seen enough similar cases) vs. **Aleatoric Uncertainty** (the image itself is blurry or noisy).
*Takeaway:* If the radiologist only sees "uncertain" cases, their own internal calibration of what "normal" looks like may drift. This is the **Complacency Trap**.

### Chapter 11 — Healthcare (ECG Overruling)

**The Core Issue:** Dataset Shift and Accountability.
When the cardiologist overrules the AI, she is acting as the final safety layer. However, if the AI was trained on a different demographic (**Dataset Shift**), its "opinion" might be fundamentally biased for this specific patient.
*Takeaway:* "Human-in-the-loop" here is a legal and ethical shield, but it only works if the human is actually paying more attention than the machine.

### Chapter 14 — Evaluation (The 95% Accuracy Model)

**The Core Issue:** The Base Rate Fallacy.
If 95% of your cases are negative, a model that always says "negative" is 95% accurate but has **zero utility**.
*Takeaway:* This is why we use **Cohen's Kappa**. It adjusts accuracy by the amount of agreement you would get by just guessing (chance). For a model that predicts one class every time, Kappa would be **0.0**, revealing the model's true lack of predictive power.
