I hereby declare that the work presented in this handbook is my own, except where it is not.

The ideas in these pages were produced with the assistance of: researchers who published papers and occasionally put them behind paywalls; annotators whose labor made the models possible but whose names appear in no acknowledgments section; open-source contributors who wrote the libraries used in every code example; and the accumulated text of human civilization, ingested and compressed by large language models that have no opinion on any of it.

The errors are mine. The insights were collaborative.

I confirm that this work has not been submitted for any other degree or professional qualification, academic prize, grant, tenure case, or LinkedIn post, except as otherwise stated.

I further confirm that no SOTA (State of the Art) comparison section was included in this work. The reader will not find a table ranking 47 methods on 12 benchmarks, with the proposed method achieving statistically significant improvements on 9 of them (†p < 0.05). This is not because such a table could not be constructed, but because its construction would be beside the point.

The point is this: the human is in the loop. Always has been. This book tries to understand what that means.
