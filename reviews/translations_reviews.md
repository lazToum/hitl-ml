# Translation & Localization Review Framework

This document outlines the review criteria for the 14 localized editions (Amharic, Arabic, German, Greek, Spanish, Farsi, French, Hausa, Hindi, Russian, Swahili, Turkish, Chinese).

## 1. Technical Terminology Consistency
*   **The "Loop" Concept:** Reviewers must ensure that "Human-in-the-loop" is translated consistently. In many languages, the English term should be retained in parentheses alongside the localized equivalent (e.g., German: *Mensch-im-Regelkreis*) to ensure students can find related literature.
*   **Active Learning:** Verify that "Active Learning" doesn't get mistranslated as general "active study" but maintains its specific machine-learning definition.
*   **RLHF:** For non-Latin scripts (Arabic, Amharic, Hindi), ensure the acronym RLHF is explained or kept in Latin script to match global research standards.

## 2. Cultural & Regional Adaptation
*   **Case Study Localism:** In **Chapter 11 (Healthcare)**, the scenarios involve specific hospital systems. Reviewers should check if the regulatory mentions (like FDA) need a local equivalent (e.g., EMA for European translations) or a note explaining they are US-specific examples.
*   **Currency & Units:** Ensure that any cost-per-decision examples (Ch 12) make sense in the target region's economic context.

## 3. Linguistic Quality
*   **Tone:** The "Summer Edition" should maintain its conversational, workbook-style tone. The translation should not become overly "textbook-heavy" or formal.
*   **RTL Layout:** For Arabic and Farsi (FA), verify that the "Spot the human" layout and the word searches are correctly oriented and legible in RTL (Right-to-Left) formatting.
